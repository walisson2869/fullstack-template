# Code of Conduct

## Our Pledge

We as contributors and maintainers pledge to make participation in this project a welcoming, respectful, and productive experience for everyone, regardless of age, body size, disability, ethnicity, gender identity and expression, level of experience, nationality, personal appearance, race, religion, or sexual identity and orientation.

## Our Standards

Examples of behavior that contribute to a positive environment:

- Using welcoming and inclusive language
- Being respectful of differing viewpoints and experiences
- Gracefully accepting constructive criticism
- Focusing on what is best for the community
- Showing empathy toward other community members

Examples of unacceptable behavior:

- Personal attacks, insults, or derogatory comments
- Public or private intimidation or threats
- Publishing others' private information without explicit permission
- Sustained disruption of discussions or contributions
- Any conduct that would reasonably be considered inappropriate in a professional setting

## Responsibilities

Project maintainers are responsible for clarifying standards of acceptable behavior and are expected to take fair and appropriate corrective action in response to any behavior they determine to be inappropriate, threatening, offensive, or harmful.

Maintainers have the right to remove, edit, or reject comments, commits, code, issues, and other contributions that are not aligned with this Code of Conduct, and to temporarily or permanently ban any contributor whose behavior is deemed harmful.

## Scope

This Code of Conduct applies within all project spaces — GitHub issues, pull requests, discussions, and any other official channel — as well as in public spaces when an individual is representing the project or its community.

## Enforcement

Instances of unacceptable behavior may be reported by opening a GitHub issue marked **[Conduct]** or by contacting the maintainers directly via the contact information listed in the repository. All reports will be reviewed and investigated promptly and fairly. Maintainers are obligated to maintain confidentiality with regard to the reporter.

Maintainers who do not follow or enforce this Code of Conduct in good faith may face temporary or permanent repercussions as determined by other project maintainers.
